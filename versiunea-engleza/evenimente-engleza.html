Events

Often, people associate the scents of freshly baked pastries, Christmas trees, or orange peel with childhood Christmas events. This happens because scents evoke memories, which are then rationalized as positive or negative. Thus, the scent travels from the nose to the olfactory bulbs through the cranial nerves, helping the brain process information. Ultimately, the olfactory bulbs access the amygdala, which plays a crucial role in storing emotional memories. Choosing a fragrance helps us create a unique event that evokes strong emotions and has a lasting memorability. Whether it's weddings, baptisms, educational or promotional events, the scent contributes to forming the first impression of the occasion. If the fragrance is right, the positive impression will remain unchanged over time.
