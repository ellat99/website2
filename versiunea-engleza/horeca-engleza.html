Horeca

In the world of hospitality, where fine details make the difference between an ordinary and a memorable experience, scent becomes an essential element in the design of hotels and the ambiance of restaurants. Unquestionable quality in design, impeccable service, and exceptional cuisine are no longer sufficient to ensure customer loyalty unless accompanied by a pleasant scent. The olfactory sense, involuntarily trained, represents a subtle yet powerful tool in creating an inviting atmosphere. People can detect a specific scent even at a concentration of one scent particle to 500 million air particles, emphasizing the significant influence scent can have on customer experience. Using a customized fragrance for hotels and restaurants will not only add uniqueness to the space but also create a pleasant and familiar atmosphere. This, subtly, will prompt consumers to return, reminding them of the pleasant states associated with that particular location. Studies indicate that relaxed guests spend more time in such places, highlighting that scent not only enhances the experience but can also generate profit, representing a significant competitive advantage for hotels and restaurants.
